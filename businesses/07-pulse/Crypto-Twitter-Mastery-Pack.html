Growth Guide
The Growth Guide ‚Äî 5 Sections
Strategy ¬∑ Scheduling ¬∑ Engagement ¬∑ Virality ¬∑ Monetization

Section 1 ‚Äî Profile Optimization

Your profile is your landing page. It either converts scrollers to followers or it doesn't. No amount of good threads will fix a bad profile.

Bio formula: [What you do] ¬∑ [For whom] ¬∑ [Proof or credential] ¬∑ [What they'll get from following]

Pinned tweet rules: Pin your best thread (high engagement), your introduction thread, or a free resource. Never pin a random hot take or an old tweet that isn't relevant.

Section 2 ‚Äî Posting Schedule

DAILY ¬∑ 30 MIN
  • 10 replies to accounts in your niche
  • 1-2 standalone tweets (observation, question)
  • Engage with replies to your content
WEEKLY
  • 2-3 threads (use templates from Part 1)
  • 1 quote tweet with your own analysis
  • 1 question tweet for engagement
BEST TIMES ¬∑ EST
  • 8-9 AM (morning commute)
  • 12-1 PM (lunch break)
  • 5-7 PM (post-work)
  • 9-11 PM (late scrollers)
THREAD TIMING
  • Post hook tweet, then rest within 15 min
  • Don't space them out
  • Algorithm treats it as one piece
  • Amplifies based on first-hour engagement

Section 3 ‚Äî Engagement Tactics

  • Add information, not just agreement. "Great take" does nothing. Add a specific point that extends the conversation.
  • Pick 10 accounts to be genuinely engaged with. Slightly larger than you, not untouchable. Consistent meaningful engagement gets noticed and gets you noticed.
  • Disagree publicly, politely. A thoughtful, specific disagreement in the replies of a large account gets enormous visibility. Never do it as a stunt ‚Äî only when you genuinely disagree.
  • Quote tweet > retweet. Retweeting adds nothing. Quote tweeting with your take adds value to both the original and your profile.
  • Build relationships, not just followers. DM people when their content helps you. Tag relevant people in your threads. Propose collabs after you've built real relationship.

Section 4 ‚Äî How to Go Viral

What "viral" actually means: A thread with 1,000 retweets is not the goal. 500 new followers who genuinely want your content is. Focus on follower quality, not vanity metrics.

What Kills Threads

  • Too long ‚Äî most threads should be 6-8 tweets. 15+ loses people unless the topic demands it
  • No clear point ‚Äî threads that meander get skimmed and abandoned
  • Generic fill-ins ‚Äî using a template with vague phrases defeats the purpose
