If you can only do one thing: post 1 quality thread per week and engage daily in replies.

Before someone buys from you, they need to:
1. Know you exist
2. Trust your expertise
3. Have a specific problem your product solves
4. Believe your product solves it better than alternatives

Your Twitter content builds 1 and 2. Your threads do the selling work for 3 and 4.

The best content for driving purchases is:

**Educational content** â€” teaches something useful, positions you as an expert
**Behind-the-scenes** â€” shows your process, builds connection and trust
**Results content** â€” shows outcomes from your frameworks (case studies, before/after)
**Free value extraction** â€” gives part of your system away free; makes people want the rest

### How to Sell Without Being Annoying
The rule: never tweet about your product more than once every 10 tweets.

The way to do it:

**Technique 1: The natural mention**

### Pricing and Positioning via Twitter
Price your product in your bio or pinned tweet only if it's a key selling point. Otherwise, let the product page do that work. On Twitter, sell the transformation and the expert, not the price.
