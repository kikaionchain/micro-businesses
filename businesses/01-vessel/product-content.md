# SECTION 2: COMMUNITY BUILDING FRAMEWORK

**The 5 Phases of Community Building**

Most projects try to run Phase 4 before they've done Phase 1. This framework keeps you honest.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks -8 to -4 Before Launch)

**What this phase is:** Building the container before you fill it.

**Goal:** A community that knows what it is and why it exists before a single outsider joins.

**Activities:**
- Write your community's founding document (1-2 pages, internal)
  - What are we building together?
  - What kind of people belong here?
  - What do we NOT tolerate?
  - What does success look like in 12 months?
- Set up channels and test them with 5-10 founding members (friends, advisors, early believers)
- Write and test all automated messages (welcome bot, rules acknowledgment, role assignments)
- Designate a Community Lead - this is a named role, not "whoever has time"
- Run 1 internal event (AMA, voice call, private workshop) before launch

**Metrics for Phase 1 completion:**
- [ ] Founding document exists
- [ ] Test community of 5-10 people active
- [ ] All automation tested and working
- [ ] Community Lead named and briefed
- [ ] 1 internal event completed

**Common mistake:** Skipping to Phase 2 because you're excited. A community with no culture becomes a price-chat. Don't skip Phase 1.

---

## Phase 2: Seeding (Weeks -4 to -1 Before Launch)

**What this phase is:** Finding and recruiting the people who will shape the community's culture.

**Goal:** 50-200 founding members who joined because they believe in the project, not because they're farming an airdrop.

**Activities:**
- Open an application or waitlist for founding members
  - Ask at least 2 real questions (what drew you here? what do you build?)
  - Manually review applications â€” reject anyone who looks like a bot or mercenary
- Begin "soft launch" content: educational posts, behind-the-scenes, building-in-public
- Host 1-2 small community calls with founding members
- Create a Founding Member designation (role, badge, or record in your docs)
- Document who your founding members are and why they matter

**Metrics for Phase 2 completion:**
- [ ] 50+ founding members admitted
- [ ] Each founding member personally welcomed by a human
- [ ] 1-2 community calls hosted
- [ ] Founding Member status formally designated

**Content to produce in this phase:**
- "What we're building" post (3x per week minimum)
- First behind-the-scenes update
- Introduction post from founding team members

---

## Phase 3: Activation (Launch Week)

**What this phase is:** The moment you open the doors. Controlled chaos with a system underneath.

**Goal:** Convert launch attention into retained community members.

**Activities:**
- Publish announcement post (see Section 4 for template)
- Pin orientation content in every channel
- All hands on deck: every team member active in community for 72 hours
- Respond to every genuine question within 2 hours
- Daily recap post: what happened today, what's coming tomorrow
- Identify 3-5 "natural connectors" in new members â€” invite them to help moderate

**What not to do in Phase 3:**
- Don't ignore the community to focus on Twitter metrics
- Don't let price/chart talk dominate â€” redirect to project substance
- Don't make promises about features you haven't built
- Don't ignore negative voices â€” address them directly and calmly

**Metrics for Phase 3 completion:**
- [ ] Announcement published and distributed
- [ ] Response time under 2 hours for first 72 hours
- [ ] Daily recap posted each day of launch week
- [ ] Natural connectors identified

---

## Phase 4: Cultivation (Weeks 2-8 Post-Launch)

**What this phase is:** Turning a crowd into a community.

**Goal:** Members who contribute, not just consume.

**Activities:**
- Launch a recurring community event (weekly AMA, bi-weekly office hours, monthly showcase)
- Create contribution pathways: how can members help? (Testing, feedback, content, ambassadors)
- Recognize contributors publicly and specifically
- Publish a community update weekly â€” progress, metrics (selectively), upcoming priorities
- Establish community feedback loops: polls, suggestion channels, direct outreach to power users

**The 80/20 rule for this phase:**
80% of your community management time should go toward the 20% of members who are active contributors. Grow the 20%, not the 80%.

**Metrics for Phase 4 completion:**
- [ ] Recurring event established and running
- [ ] At least 3 contribution pathways defined
- [ ] 5+ members publicly recognized as contributors
- [ ] Weekly community update cadence established

---

## Phase 5: Scaling (Month 3 Onward)

**What this phase is:** Building systems that outlast your direct involvement.

**Goal:** A community that generates its own culture, content, and new members.

**Activities:**
- Formalize an Ambassador Program
  - Clear criteria for becoming an ambassador
  - Defined responsibilities (not vague "spread the word")
  - Real benefits (access, recognition, occasional compensation)
- Document your community's culture in writing â€” things that would be lost if your core team disappeared
- Delegate moderation fully to trusted community members
- Create onboarding content that doesn't require a team member to deliver
- Begin community-led initiatives: hackathons, content contests, local meetups

**Questions to ask yourself at Phase 5:**
- If I stepped back for 2 weeks, would the community survive?
- Do members recruit new members organically?
- Does the community have inside jokes, rituals, and traditions of its own?

**Metrics for Phase 5 completion:**
- [ ] Ambassador Program launched with 5+ ambassadors
- [ ] Culture document written
- [ ] At least 1 community-led initiative completed
- [ ] Moderation fully delegated

# APPENDIX: QUICK REFERENCE CARD

**The 5 rules of a healthy launch:**

1. **Over-communicate.** Your community would rather hear too much than wonder if you've disappeared.
2. **Answer the hard questions first.** If you know something is going to be asked, answer it before they ask.
3. **Specificity beats hype.** "We shipped X to Y users, and here's what we learned" > "Big things coming soon!!!"
4. **Culture is content.** Your inside jokes, your rituals, your language â€” these are not soft. They are the product.
5. **Speed is generosity.** Responding fast to your community is a form of respect. Slow responses tell people you don't care.
