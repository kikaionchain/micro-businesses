### Tactic 3: Be Visibly Active
For the first 2 weeks, someone from your team (or you) needs to respond to EVERY message in your Discord within 2 hours. Every single one.

### Tactic 4: The Daily Thread
Every day in #general, post a conversation starter. Not an announcement. A question.

Examples:
- "What's one protocol you've been impressed by this week and why?"
- "Unpopular opinion time: which L1 is actually overrated?"
- "What's your biggest mistake in crypto that you'd warn others about?"

These threads generate conversation, help you understand your audience, and give new members something to engage with immediately.

## Phase 1 Twitter Content Strategy

**Posting frequency:** 1x per day minimum, 3x per day maximum

**Content mix:**

## Phase 2 Moderation Guide

As your community grows, moderation becomes a real job. Set up:

**Auto-moderation rules (Carl-bot or Dyno):**
- Block Discord invite links (except from mods)
- Block links to known scam sites
- Timeout users who post @everyone without permission
- Filter excessive caps (set to 70%+ caps triggers warning)
- Timeout users for repeated identical messages

**Human moderation:**
- Designate at least 2 moderators by 300 members
- Create a #mod-logs channel (private, team only) to track actions taken
- Ban policy: 1st offense = warning, 2nd = 24h timeout, 3rd = permanent ban (unless it's spam/scam = immediate permanent)
- Create a #report channel where members can flag issues

**Moderator Handbook (share this with your mods):**
```
MODERATOR PRINCIPLES

1. Always assume positive intent first. Most confusion is not malice.
2. Enforce rules consistently. Inconsistency destroys trust.
3. Never argue publicly with members. Handle escalations in DMs.
4. When in doubt, consult the team before acting.
5. Document everything in #mod-logs.
6. Take breaks. Moderation is emotionally taxing. You're human.
7. Your job is to protect the culture, not to control the conversation.
```

## Phase 2 Retention Strategies

Getting members is easy. Keeping them is the real work.

**Top retention tactics:**

1. **The 7-day check-in:** When someone hasn't posted in 7 days, have a bot (or mod) send them a friendly DM asking if they have any questions. This reactivates ~15% of inactive members.

2. **The contributor spotlight:** Every week, feature a community member who did something great. Post in #announcements: "This week's community MVP is @[handle] for [specific thing they did]. Go follow them."

3. **Progress updates:** Share real metrics with your community. When people feel like they're watching something grow, they stay. "We hit 500 members today. A year ago there were 3 of us. Thanks for being here."

4. **Community events that aren't just AMAs:**
   - Weekly trivia (crypto topics, use a trivia bot)
   - Trading competitions with small prizes
   - "Best portfolio screenshot" contests
   - Community-written content projects (collaborative research reports)
   - Voice hangouts (no agenda, just vibe)

5. **Earn rewards:** Give members ways to earn things for contributing. Points, roles, access, allocations. Make contribution feel rewarding, not just expected.

- Once a quarter, hold a structured community vote on a real decision
- Even if you're centralized, the process creates ownership feeling

## Tactic 14: Content Machine

Phase 3 requires more content than one person can produce. Build a content machine:

**Systems to build:**

1. **Community Content Program:** Pay or reward members for creating content about your project. Set a template (thread structure, key talking points, required disclosures). Amplify the best ones from your main account.

2. **Thread Bank:** A Notion database of thread ideas, half-written threads, and past viral threads. Anyone on your team can pick one up and ship it.

3. **Media Kit:** Public folder with logos, screenshots, graphics. Lower the friction for anyone wanting to write about you.

4. **Press / Research Page:** A single page with key stats, team bios, and a contact for journalists. One journalist writing about you can bring hundreds of new members.

## Phase 3 Retention Strategies

At 10,000 members, active rate will naturally drop. Your goal isn't 100% engagement â€” it's a core of 500+ highly active members surrounded by thousands of warm ones.

**Retention at scale:**

2. **Memory-making:** The best communities have inside jokes, legendary moments, memorable events. Be intentional about creating these. A controversial AMA, an unexpected announcement, a crazy trade that worked out.

3. **Cross-chain visibility:** Expand to where your target audience lives. Telegram for Asian markets. LinkedIn for institutional angles. Never fully abandon a platform â€” just distribute effort.

4. **Mental model for retention:** Every member is asking "why should I come back tomorrow?" Your job is to ensure tomorrow always has an answer.

# APPENDIX C: THE 10 COMMANDMENTS OF CRYPTO COMMUNITY BUILDING

1. **Culture before size.** 100 engaged believers beat 10,000 ghost accounts every time.

2. **Show up daily.** Consistency builds trust faster than any single viral moment.

3. **Answer every question.** Especially early on. Unanswered questions become doubts.

4. **Celebrate the community.** It's not your community. It's theirs. Act like it.

5. **Be honest about setbacks.** If something breaks or a launch gets delayed, say so first. Your community forgives honesty. They don't forgive silence.

6. **Moderate with values.** Remove bad actors fast and publicly. Set the standard.

7. **Never buy fake members.** The short-term number isn't worth the long-term reputation damage.

8. **Let the community lead sometimes.** The best communities have grassroots initiatives from members, not just top-down content from founders.

9. **Your community is your moat.** When the market turns bear and the fair-weather holders leave, your core community stays. Invest in them accordingly.

10. **Play long-term games.** Community is a 3-year asset. Don't sacrifice trust for a short-term growth hack.
