How To Use
Read This First
This guide is split into three phases. Each phase covers what to focus on, what to avoid, specific tactics, scripts you can use today, metrics to track, and when to move to the next phase.

Important: Don't skip phases. The mistake most founders make is jumping to Phase 3 tactics ‚Äî paid growth, influencer deals, big events ‚Äî before they've built the foundation. You'll get numbers but no engagement. You'll have 10,000 members and 12 daily active users.
Community is the only moat in crypto that can't be forked. A competitor can copy your tokenomics. They can hire your developers. They can't steal your community's trust. That's why this work matters more than most founders realize ‚Äî and why doing it wrong is so costly.

Phase 1
0 ‚Üí 100 Members: The Foundation
You're not building a community yet. You're building a culture.
At 0-100, every person who joins will define what your community becomes. They'll set the tone, establish the norms, and either attract or repel the next 900 people. Be more selective than you think you should be.

PHASE 1 TACTICS

1
Manual Outreach ‚Äî Yes, Manual
Your first 50 members should be hand-invited. Not spam blasted. Go through your personal network on Twitter, relevant Discord servers, Reddit communities (r/CryptoCurrency, r/ethfinance, niche subreddits), Telegram groups, and LinkedIn. Engage genuinely first. Mention your project second.
// The DM That Works Hey [NAME], I saw your [tweet/post/comment] about [TOPIC] ‚Äî really good take. I'm building [ONE SENTENCE ABOUT PROJECT]. We just opened our Discord and I'm being selective about who I invite early ‚Äî looking for people who care about [NICHE]. Would you be interested in joining? Zero spam, no hype BS, just a small group of people working through this stuff seriously. [DISCORD LINK]
What doesn't work: Mass DMs with no personalization. Don't do it.
2
The Founding Member Program
Create a "Founding Member" role for your first 100 members. Give them a special Discord role with a unique badge, their name in your founding members shoutout post, early access to features, and the feeling of being part of something before it was big.
// Founding Member Announcement üå± FOUNDING MEMBER PROGRAM The first 100 people to join [PROJECT] Discord and introduce themselves get the üèÜ Founding Member role ‚Äî forever. 3
Be Visibly Active
For the first 2 weeks, someone from your team must respond to EVERY message in your Discord within 2 hours. Every single one. This is non-negotiable at this stage. It signals: "Real people built this. They care." Nothing kills an early community faster than unanswered messages.
4
The Daily Thread
Every day in #general, post a conversation starter. Not an announcement. A question. These generate conversation, help you understand your audience, and give new members something to engage with immediately.
// Daily Thread Examples "What's one protocol you've been impressed by this week and why?" "Unpopular opinion time: which L1 is actually overrated?" "What's your biggest mistake in crypto that you'd warn others about?" "If you had to put your entire portfolio into one token for 2 years, what would it be?" "Name a project that died that you still think had a good idea." "What would make you move your liquidity tomorrow?"

  • Have a moderator handle the queue
  • Record and share a text summary after

MODERATION GUIDE ‚Äî PHASE 2

Moderator Principles (Share with Your Mods)

1.
Always assume positive intent first. Most confusion is not malice.
2.
Enforce rules consistently. Inconsistency destroys trust more than any single bad actor.
3.
Never argue publicly with members. Handle escalations in DMs.
4.
When in doubt, consult the team before acting.
5.
Document everything in #mod-logs.
6.
Take breaks. Moderation is emotionally taxing. You're human.
7.
Your job is to protect the culture, not to control the conversation.

Ban policy: 1st offense = warning ¬∑ 2nd = 24h timeout ¬∑ 3rd = permanent ban ¬∑ Spam/scam = immediate permanent ban, no warnings.

RETENTION ‚Äî TOP 5 TACTICS FOR PHASE 2

1. The 7-day check-in: When someone hasn't posted in 7 days, have a bot or mod send them a friendly DM. This reactivates ~15% of inactive members.

2. The contributor spotlight: Every week, feature a community member who did something great. Specific praise over generic recognition.

3. Progress updates: Share real metrics. When people feel like they're watching something grow, they stay.

4. Community events beyond AMAs: Weekly trivia, trading competitions, best portfolio screenshot contests, voice hangouts with no agenda.

5. Earn rewards: Give members ways to earn things for contributing ‚Äî points, roles, access, allocations. Make contribution feel rewarding, not expected.

Big Event Playbook
5 event types that work at 1K+ members:
1. Monthly Town Hall (AMA 2.0) ‚Äî Full team on voice. Pre-submitted + live questions. Record for YouTube/podcast. Target: 200+ live attendees.

2. Monthly Trading Competition ‚Äî Partner with a DEX for prize pool. Leaderboard posted publicly. Target: 100+ participants.

3. Community Research Report ‚Äî Ambassadors collaborate on a research report. Community submits insights. Published publicly. Creates content, recognition, and depth signal.

4. Twitter Spaces (2x/month) ‚Äî Invite 1-2 guests from relevant projects. Promote 3 days in advance. Target: 300+ listeners.

5. Governance Day (quarterly) ‚Äî Structured community vote on a real decision. Announce result publicly. Explain how community input influenced it. Creates ownership feeling.
13
KOL Strategy
At Phase 3, you have the credibility to approach Key Opinion Leaders properly. Build the relationship before asking for anything. Offer exclusive access or early alpha rather than just money. Co-create content rather than just getting a shoutout.
// KOL Outreach Template Hey [NAME], I've been following your content on [topic] for a while ‚Äî your take on [specific recent piece] was one of the most useful things I read this month. I'm building [PROJECT] ‚Äî [one sentence]. We're at [X] Discord members and [Y] Twitter followers. Our community is genuinely engaged ‚Äî [specific proof: "our weekly AMAs get 300+ live attendees"]. I'd love to explore a collab. Not a paid shill ‚Äî actual co-created content on [topic both audiences care about]. You'd get [specific benefit], we'd get [specific benefit]. No rush at all ‚Äî just wanted to put it out there.
Rule: Find KOLs who genuinely align with your niche. Don't pay a meme KOL to shill a DeFi protocol.
14
Content Machine
Phase 3 requires more content than one person can produce. Build systems:
Community Content Program: Pay or reward members for creating content about your project. Set a template. Amplify the best from your main account.

Thread Bank: A database of thread ideas, half-written threads, and past viral threads. Anyone on your team can pick one up.

Media Kit: Public folder with logos, screenshots, graphics. Lower the friction for anyone wanting to write about you.

Press / Research Page: Single page with key stats, team bios, and a media contact. One journalist covering you can bring hundreds of new members.

RETENTION AT SCALE

At 10,000 members, active rate will naturally drop. Your goal isn't 100% engagement ‚Äî it's a core of 500+ highly active members surrounded by thousands of warm ones.
